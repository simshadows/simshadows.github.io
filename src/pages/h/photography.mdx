## Section 2a: Core Technical Concepts

### Exposure Triangle

#### Shutter Speed (t)

Slower shutter speed &#8594; more exposure, more motion blur.


#### Aperture (N)

Smaller f-stop number &#8594; larger aperture &#8594; more exposure, shallower depth of field.

The f-stop (<Eq code="N"/>) is a dimensionless ratio:

<DisplayLatex code="
    N
    = \frac{f}{D}
    = \frac{\text{focal length}}{\text{diameter of entrance pupil (effective aperture)}}
" />

Often written in the format "f/N", which usefully forms an expression of <Eq code="D"/>.

Recall the area of a circle (<Eq code="A"/>) as a function of radius (<Eq code="r"/>) or diameter (<Eq code="D"/>):

<DisplayLatex code="
    A = \pi r^2 = \frac{1}{4} \pi D^2
    \qquad\qquad
    A \propto D^2 \propto \sqrt{N}
" />

#### ISO

Higher ISO number &#8594; more exposure, and *usually* more noise.


#### Exposure Value (EV)

EV represents the overall exposure of the image as a function of <Eq code="t"/> and <Eq code="N"/>. Commonly referred to as *stops of light*.

It's a base-2 logarithm, so a difference of <Eq code="\pm 1 \text{EV}"/> represents a doubling or halving of light hitting the camera sensor:

<DisplayLatex code="
    \text{EV}
    = \log_2{\frac{N^2}{t}}
    = 2 \log_2{N} - \log_2{t}
"/>
